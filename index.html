Global HIV Burden Treatment Coverage Analysis

This Power BI project analyses global HIV trends from 2000 to 2024, focusing on new infections, HIV-related deaths, people living with HIV, ART coverage, death rate, survival rate, and country-level risk. The dataset was cleaned, modelled into a star schema, and transformed into an interactive dashboard with slicers for year and continent. The dashboard provides key public health insights, including 2024 HIV burden, treatment coverage, mortality outcomes, YoY changes, and countries requiring closer intervention. The analysis shows that while HIV deaths and new infections have declined over time, treatment access remains uneven across countries. This project demonstrates skills in Power BI, Power Query, data modelling, DAX, KPI development, dashboard design, and data storytelling.

Key Insights:
• In 2024, 35.23M people were living with HIV globally.
• New infections reached 984K, while HIV-related deaths were 523K..
• Death rate was 1.49%, while survival rate was 98.51%.
• From 2000 to 2024, new infections declined by 59.02% and HIV deaths declined by 65.50%.
• South Africa had the highest HIV burden in the 2024.
• Treatment access remains uneven across countries, especially among high-infection countries.

VitalCare Performance Analysis

The VitalCare Health Group report revealed important year-on-year changes across operational, clinical, and financial performance. Across all three dashboard pages, the analysis compared 2023 and 2024 performance to help stakeholders understand whether patient demand, clinical outcomes, resource utilisation, and financial sustainability improved or declined. The Overview dashboard highlighted overall hospital activity, admissions trends, patient distribution, revenue movement, average length of stay, and readmission performance. The Clinical Analysis dashboard provided deeper insight into patient outcomes, bed occupancy, recovery rate, mortality rate, diagnosis patterns, and department-level clinical pressure. The Financial Analysis dashboard examined revenue, cost, profit, profit margin, revenue per patient, doctor contribution, and department-level financial performance. Together, the three-page report gives healthcare stakeholders a clear view of how operational demand, clinical performance, and financial outcomes are connected, supporting better decision-making around capacity planning, patient care improvement, and financial sustainability.

Key Findings:
• VitalCare Health Group showed improved performance between 2023 and 2024, with admissions increasing by 4.08%, total patients by 3.43%, revenue by 5.75%, and profit by 4.70%.
• Clinical performance also improved, with mortality rate reducing by 15.77% and average length of stay slightly decreasing from 5.03 to 4.99 days. However, readmission rate increased by 8.68%, highlighting the need for stronger discharge planning and patient follow-up.
• Financially, revenue and profit improved, but cost increased slightly faster than revenue, causing profit margin to decline from 14.82% to 14.67%. The report also identified General Surgery as the highest admission department and Oncology as the strongest revenue and profit contributor.


Tools Used:
• Power BI • Power Query • DAX • Data Modelling • KPI Reporting • Interactive Dashboard Design
