Green Valley Medical Centre Performance Analysis

An Excel-based healthcare analytics project analysing 400 patient records to evaluate recovery outcomes, treatment effectiveness, departmental performance, treatment cost, and patient demographics.

The interactive dashboard tracks total patients, average recovery score, average treatment cost, and average patient age, with slicers for department, doctor, treatment type, and gender.

Key Insights:
• Average recovery score was 75.
• Average treatment cost was £5,052.
• Dermatology and General Medicine recorded the strongest recovery performance.
• Cardiology showed lower recovery outcomes.
• Surgery achieved the best treatment recovery score.
• Low-cost treatment patients made up 80% of the distribution.

Global HIV Burden Treatment Coverage Analysis

A Power BI public health analytics project analysing global HIV trends from 2000 to 2024, focusing on new infections, HIV-related deaths, people living with HIV, ART coverage, death rate, survival rate, and country-level risk.

The dataset was cleaned, modelled into a star schema, and transformed into an interactive dashboard with slicers for year and continent.

Key Insights:
• In 2024, 35.23M people were living with HIV globally.
• New infections reached 984K, while HIV-related deaths were 523K.
• Death rate was 1.49%, while survival rate was 98.51%.
• From 2000 to 2024, new infections declined by 59.02% and HIV deaths declined by 65.50%.
• South Africa had the highest HIV burden in 2024.
• Treatment access remains uneven across countries, especially among high-infection countries.

VitalCare Performance Analysis

A three-page Power BI healthcare dashboard comparing 2023 and 2024 performance across operational, clinical, and financial outcomes. The report helps stakeholders understand how patient demand, clinical outcomes, resource utilisation, and financial sustainability changed year-on-year.

The report includes an Overview page, Clinical Analysis page, and Financial Analysis page, covering admissions, patient trends, revenue, length of stay, readmissions, clinical outcomes, bed occupancy, mortality rate, profit, cost, and department-level performance.

Key Findings:
• Admissions increased by 4.08%.
• Total patients increased by 3.43%.
• Revenue increased by 5.75%, while profit increased by 4.70%.
• Mortality rate reduced by 15.77%.
• Readmission rate increased by 8.68%, highlighting the need for stronger discharge planning and patient follow-up.
• Profit margin declined from 14.82% to 14.67% because cost increased slightly faster than revenue.
• General Surgery had the highest admissions, while Oncology was the strongest revenue and profit contributor.
